Biography

Learn the life of the reputed "Pac Man"

Profile

Manny Pacquiao, born on December 17, 1978, in Kibawe, Bukidnon, Philippines, is a Filipino professional boxer and politician. Known for his speed, power, and relentless work ethic, he is the only boxer in history to win world titles in eight different weight classes.

Personal Life and Legacy

Beyond the ring, Pacquiao has built a legacy as a philanthropist and public servant. He has served as a senator in the Philippines, where he actively shares his opinions on public matters, including policies and social issues, and is dedicated to various humanitarian causes.

Notable Achievements

Pacquiao is the only boxer in history to win world titles in eight different weight classes. This incredible achievement, spanning from flyweight (112 pounds) to super welterweight (154 pounds), highlights his adaptability and skill across various weight divisions.

Pacquiao achieved the remarkable feat of winning world titles in four different decades (1990s, 2000s, 2010s, and 2020s). This showcases his longevity and resilience at the elite level of boxing, adapting to different eras of the sport.

Pacquiao won the prestigious Fighter of the Year award multiple times (2006, 2008, 2009) for his impressive victories and exceptional performances in the ring. These accolades solidified his reputation as one of the greatest fighters in the sport.

Personal Milestones

Early Life

Manny grew up in a poor family, leaving home at a young age to support his family through odd jobs and boxing.

Political Career

Elected as a senator in the Philippines in 2016, Manny focused on issues such as anti-corruption and poverty reduction.

Philanthropy

Manny is actively involved in humanitarian work, including relief efforts and building homes for the poor.
